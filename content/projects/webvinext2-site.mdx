This project is the current evolution of my personal site: a portfolio platform that combines content publishing, project storytelling, and a Worker-friendly delivery model.

It takes the strongest ideas from the previous Astro version - clear positioning, structured writing, and a project-first narrative - and rebuilds them on a runtime that is better aligned with how I want to ship and iterate.

## Goals

- Replace placeholder positioning with a clearer engineering narrative
- Keep the content model explicit so new posts and projects stay easy to publish
- Make preview, build, and deployment behavior reliable in the Cloudflare Worker runtime
- Treat visual design and platform architecture as parts of the same product surface

## What Changed

- Reworked the site around generated MDX modules instead of runtime frontmatter parsing
- Strengthened the homepage, shell, and cards with a more deliberate editorial visual language
- Kept validation in the content layer so publishing stays structured instead of ad hoc
- Reduced platform friction by fixing Worker preview compatibility

## Outcome

The result is not just a nicer portfolio. It is a stronger base for ongoing writing, case studies, and future product experiments.
