As AI-assisted systems become more capable, they also need better ways to persist context without adding unnecessary infrastructure complexity.

One of the most interesting directions I explored was pairing Cloudflare Workers with D1 so agents could store and retrieve structured context close to where the compute runs.

## Why D1 Was Interesting

D1 offers a simple edge-friendly persistence model. For agent workflows, that makes it useful for lightweight state, contextual lookups, and durable traces of what happened between interactions.

## What the Prototype Proved

In practice, the combination was useful for small retrieval flows where an agent needed to pull context before generating a response. The setup stayed much simpler than a traditional database-backed service while still giving me structured storage.

## Key Takeaways

- Edge-native persistence changes how you think about agent state
- Lightweight databases are often enough for orchestration and context lookup
- The hardest part is not storage - it is designing clear boundaries for what the agent should remember and why

This is still an active area of exploration, but it is already shaping how I think about AI workflows at the edge.
